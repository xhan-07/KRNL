About Jaguars

The jaguar (Panthera onca) is the largest cat species in the Americas and the third-largest in the world after tigers and lions.

Jaguars are known for their powerful build and distinctive coat pattern of rosettes (rose-like markings).

These magnificent cats can weigh between 100-250 pounds (45-113 kg) and measure up to 6 feet (1.8 m) in length, not including their tail.

Habitat

Jaguars are found in a variety of habitats including:

  • Rainforests
  • Swamps
  • Grasslands
  • Scrublands
  • Deserts

Their range extends from the southwestern United States through Mexico and Central America into South America, including much of the Amazon basin.

Diet and Hunting

Jaguars are apex predators with a diverse diet that includes:

  • Deer
  • Peccaries
  • Capybaras
  • Tapirs
  • Fish
  • Turtles
  • Caimans

They are known for their powerful bite which allows them to pierce the skulls or shells of their prey.

Conservation Status

Jaguars are currently classified as Near Threatened by the IUCN Red List.

Major threats to jaguars include:

  • Habitat loss due to deforestation
  • Human-wildlife conflict
  • Illegal hunting for their pelts
  • Climate change

Conservation efforts are underway throughout their range to protect these magnificent cats.

Fun Facts About Jaguars

  • Jaguars are excellent swimmers and often hunt in water
  • They have the strongest bite of all big cats relative to their size
  • Black panthers are actually jaguars or leopards with melanism
  • Jaguars were revered in many ancient Mesoamerican cultures
  • They are solitary animals except during mating season
